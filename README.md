# TOP-Calculator
The TOP Calculator Project:

This took me a while to do. I actually began to lose hope with how
paralyzed I felt starting this project. However, bit by bit I 
added pieces and moved pieces around, and refactored. Often, I would
have to go back and reread something from MDN or a similar site, and
even then sometimes I would need to see examples. But, with time and 
effort this finally came together. Despite many hours and an entire
lack of committing to git as TOP recommends, I have finally made
a calculator that is basic in functionality, but at least throws an
error and resets the display if the user attempts to divide by zero.

The lesson here for my future self, since no one is likely to ever
check out this readme is this: Take it one step at a time. You 
cannot hold the entire project in your head. Complete what you know
and what is easy first (HTML, CSS), and then work on JS. Once there,
create a single function to do even a single piece of what you
want. From there, expand outward logically. Do not be afraid to
refactor! Even if this means refactoring the entire project top to
bottom, just do it. The second time around on this calculator 
project really made me realize where my previous mistakes in logic
were and where I might improve the readability of my code. 

This is a long road, and the foundations course has been difficult,
but there is a part of me that is driven to learn and to improve
my problem solving abilities. I look forward to the challenge.
